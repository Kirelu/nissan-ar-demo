Nissan Skyline R34 GTR (2002)

The Nissan Skyline R34 GT-R is a sports car and grand tourer produced by Nissan from 1999 to 2002. It was the culmination of the Skyline GT-R legacy before being succeeded by the standalone GT-R model.

Specifications

  • Engine: 2.6L RB26DETT Twin-Turbo Inline-6
  • Power: 276 HP (officially, but actually more)
  • Torque: 397 Nm @ 4,400 rpm
  • Transmission: 6-speed manual
  • Drive System: ATTESA E-TS All-Wheel Drive
  • 0-60 mph: 4.8 seconds
  • Top Speed: 156 mph (electronically limited)

Cultural Impact

The R34 GT-R gained worldwide fame through its appearances in video games like Gran Turismo and movies like "The Fast and the Furious" series. It has become one of the most iconic Japanese sports cars ever produced.

Collectible Status

Due to its limited production numbers and legendary status, the R34 GT-R has become highly collectible, with prices steadily increasing over the years. In the United States, it only became legal to import in 2024 under the 25-year import rule.
